01 — Problem
Why Synthetic
Data?
  • Pedestrian detection fails in the conditions that matter most — occlusion, low-light, rare poses, crowd interactions
  • Real training data is expensive, privacy-regulated, and cannot capture rare safety-critical events
  • Synthetic simulation generates automatic, precise annotations with full scene control
  • But a synthetic-to-real domain gap limits direct usefulness — texture, lighting, and occlusion differ fundamentally from the real world

02 — Research Gap
  • Prior work dominated by Faster R-CNN; YOLOv8n anchor-free architecture untested in synthetic-to-real transfer
  • Does the integration mechanism — not just the data — determine the outcome?
🎯 Research Aim

Determine whether synthetic pedestrian data improves YOLOv8n real-world performance, and identify which integration strategy is most effective under realistic CPU constraints.

★ Key Finding
The 70:30 real-dominated strategy achieves the highest mAP50 (0.666, +12.7%) with simultaneous gains in both Precision (+6.8%) and Recall (+9.8%) — indicating an upward shift of the PR curve, not merely a threshold adjustment. This is the first controlled evidence of this pattern in an anchor-free YOLOv8n architecture.
🎓 Conclusions
  • Three of five strategies outperform the real-only baseline when trained to full convergence
  • 70:30 mixing is the most effective data-level strategy; synthetic pretraining achieves comparable mAP50 via a different mechanism
  • Synthetic-only training is unusable (mAP50 = 0.220, FN rate = 92%) — domain gap requires real-data grounding
  • The integration method, not synthetic data alone, determines the magnitude of gain
🌍 Societal Impact
  • Improved pedestrian detection under occlusion and scale variation contributes directly to AV and ADAS safety
  • Synthetic augmentation reduces dependence on expensive, privacy-sensitive real-world recording
  • CPU-feasible methodology demonstrates that safety-critical AI research is accessible beyond GPU-scale infrastructure
  • Results are comparative evidence only — not a basis for deployment without further safety validation (ISO 26262 / ISO 21448)
🔭 Future Work
  • Apply GAN-based or diffusion style transfer to reduce the synthetic-to-real texture gap without architecture changes
  • Scale to larger YOLOv8 variants (s, m, l) and GPU-based training with expanded real-world corpora
  • Investigate panoramic-specific distortion handling for 360° camera deployments using SYNTHIA-PANO
